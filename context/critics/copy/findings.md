# Findings — Week 2026-04-23

## Issues

- [CRITICAL] Hero H1 "YOUR PRACTICE. THEIR PLAYBOOK. PATIENTS NOTICE." fails one-read clarity — "THEIR" has no clear referent on a cold read: it could be the practitioners' rogue individual playbooks (the problem) or Richer Health's proprietary playbook (the solution). The three fragments resolve only after reading the subheadline, violating the one-read test for a hero headline. — Hero section, H1 text.

- [HIGH] Hero subheadline is feature-framed, not outcome-framed — "One clinical framework so every patient gets the same standard of care, no matter who they see" leads with the tool (the framework) rather than the operator's outcome (patients stop leaving; revenue stops walking out). This is the second-most benefit-critical position on the page. — Hero section, subheadline.

- [HIGH] Stats block carries no source, no sample size, and no stated baseline — "67% of patients leave over inconsistent clinical advice," "3× more patients retained with unified protocols," and "$240K avg revenue lost per practitioner from patient churn" are persuasive but unattributed. "Avg" without N= is weasel-level specificity for B2B clinical operators who will Google these numbers. — "The Hidden Cost" section, stat bar.

- [HIGH] "N&D Certification" used in the closing CTA with zero on-page definition — "30 minutes to see if the N&D Certification can turn your team into one team." N&D = Nutrition & Detoxification (per business context), but no bracket-definition or expansion appears anywhere on the page. A warm-curious audience sees an opaque acronym at the exact moment they are asked to act. — Final CTA section.

- [HIGH] Offer stack is entirely absent — nowhere on the page does copy state the certification's delivery format (in-person / virtual / hybrid), practitioner count limits, completion timeline, or investment level. Visitors cannot qualify themselves or their budget before booking; the call bears 100% of the qualification load. — Steps section and final CTA section.

- [MEDIUM] Section header "THE PATH TO MASTERY" does not signal the section content — a scannable reader encounters an aspirational phrase with no indication that a numbered 4-step process follows. A header like "HOW CERTIFICATION WORKS — 4 STEPS" carries the same tone with ten times the scanability. — Steps / process section.

- [MEDIUM] Step 02 copy combines a passive construction with an unqualified promise — "Built around your patient load" has no agent (we? the platform?). "No disruption to revenue" is a strong claim left dangling: compared to what baseline, over what period, and guaranteed by what mechanism? — Step 02 card copy.

- [MEDIUM] Step 04 ongoing support is vague — "Protocol updates as the science evolves. Case support so quality never drifts back to where it was." No format (live calls, async, written updates), cadence (monthly, quarterly), or access method is stated. This is a selling point — vagueness deflates it. — Step 04 card copy.

- [MEDIUM] "Trained under Charlotte Gerson, Dr. T. Colin Campbell, and Dr. Neal Barnard" is passive and drops Nicolette as agent — the surrounding paragraph is first-person and punchy ("I wrote… I built…"); this sentence reverts to passive, weakening the credential claim. Should read "I trained under…" — Founder bio section.

- [MEDIUM] ICP qualifier appears only at the very bottom of the page — "For clinical group directors ready to unify their team" is the page's sole explicit audience signal, placed in the final CTA section. A solo practitioner reads the entire page before self-filtering. Moving a qualifier (e.g., "Built for multi-practitioner clinics") to the hero subtext reduces unqualified calls and tightens message-to-market match on first scroll. — Final CTA section, audience qualifier line.

## Patterns observed

- Week 1 baseline — no prior runs. Patterns will accumulate from next cycle.
- First-run signal: the page's three strongest assets (punchy problem framing in "Practitioner Roulette is Killing Retention," credible founder credentials, Tony Robbins social proof) are undermined by a hero headline that misfires on clarity and an offer stack that leaves the audience with no decision-making inputs before the CTA. Specificity is the page's primary debt.

## Out of scope

- [brand-voice] Step card labels ("Custom Onboarding Plan," "All-In Team Training," "Mastery + Mentorship") are clear in meaning but flat in personality relative to the punchy section headers — voice calibration, not copy mechanics.
- [conversion] CTA button frequency and placement across the three CTA instances; Tony Robbins testimonial position relative to conversion flow.
- [fh-compliance] Stats ("67%," "$240K avg") and health outcome language ("reversing chronic disease," "Cancer, diabetes, autoimmune") may require sourcing or disclaimer given business context note that Nicolette Richer is not a medical doctor.
- [visual] Hero background image (Pacific Northwest forest ferns) has no visible connection to clinical practice or team certification — relevance gap is a visual/creative call.
