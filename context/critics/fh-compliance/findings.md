# Findings — Week 2026-04-23

## Issues

- [CRITICAL] DSocSci / non-MD disclaimer is entirely absent from the page — Nicolette is called "FOUNDER & MASTER CLINICIAN" and health-outcome claims appear throughout, yet no disclaimer stating "Dr. Nicolette Richer holds a Doctorate in Social Sciences (DSocSci). She is not a licensed medical doctor." appears anywhere on the LP. Required wherever health outcomes are discussed. — evidence: rendered LP, no matching text found in any section

- [CRITICAL] Disease-reversal claim, unhedged — "25 years reversing chronic disease. Cancer, diabetes, autoimmune, mental health." The verb "reversing" applied to named diseases (especially cancer) constitutes a therapeutic cure/treatment claim that requires Rx-drug-style substantiation under Health Canada / FTC guidelines. No hedge ("may support", "research suggests") present. — evidence: FOUNDER section hero copy

- [CRITICAL] "72K+ Patients Treated" — the word "Treated" (capital T, stat callout) implies a licensed medical treatment relationship. DSocSci practitioners see clients, not patients, and do not "treat" in the legal/clinical sense. Risk: implies unlicensed medical practice. — evidence: stat row beneath founder bio

- [CRITICAL] Medical-advice disclaimer absent from footer and page body — "This is not medical advice. Consult your physician before making changes to your health regimen." appears nowhere on the LP. Required near any protocol-like content and in the footer. — evidence: footer renders only Privacy · Terms · Clinical Standards · Contact · © 2026

- [HIGH] "FOUNDER & MASTER CLINICIAN" title — "Master Clinician" carries an implied licensed-medical-practitioner meaning. Combined with disease-reversal claims and the absence of a DSocSci disclosure, this title materially elevates the impression that Nicolette holds a medical licence. — evidence: founder section subtitle

- [HIGH] "Protocol that works in the real world, with real patients, under real pressure." — "protocol" language (clinical term of art) used in direct sentence proximity to the cancer/diabetes/autoimmune disease claims amplifies the impression of licensed clinical disease treatment. Must be re-framed or accompanied by scope-of-practice disclosure. — evidence: founder bio paragraph

- [MEDIUM] Statistic "67% of patients leave over inconsistent clinical advice" — presented as fact with no source citation. Unsubstantiated advertising statistics breach general advertising-law truthfulness requirements (ASC Code, FTC guidelines). — evidence: "The Hidden Cost" section stat row

- [MEDIUM] Statistic "3× more patients retained with unified protocols" — same issue as above; no citation or methodology disclosed. — evidence: "The Hidden Cost" section stat row

- [MEDIUM] Headline "THEORY KILLS." — hyperbolic language in a clinical health context. "Kills" as a consequence of choosing theory over protocol implies life-or-death medical negligence, a framing that amplifies perceived medical authority beyond DSocSci scope and could attract regulatory scrutiny. — evidence: founder section H2 headline

- [LOW] Training lineage ("Trained under Charlotte Gerson, Dr. T. Colin Campbell, and Dr. Neal Barnard") cited without any credential-scope qualifier — all three are associated with cancer and chronic-disease treatment paradigms. Without the DSocSci disclaimer nearby, this passage deepens the impression that Nicolette practises licensed cancer/disease treatment. — evidence: founder bio paragraph

## Patterns observed

- Week 1 (baseline): The LP makes no scope-of-practice disclosure anywhere. The pattern of combining disease names (cancer, diabetes, autoimmune) with action verbs ("reversing", "treated", "protocol") without hedges or disclaimers runs throughout the founder bio section. This is a systemic framing problem, not isolated phrasing.
- The gap between the B2B angle (selling team certification to clinic owners) and the disease-reversal credibility claims creates a structural compliance tension: the LP borrows clinical-disease language to build authority but is selling a training/certification product, which makes the unhedged claims harder to substantiate.
